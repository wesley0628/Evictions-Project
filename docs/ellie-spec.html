·         Background:

As you may know, throughout most of the pandemic, there have been several eviction moratoriums.  There is concern that once this lifts, there will be a wave of evictions.

 

We at the City want to monitor these in real time, which we can do because the circuit court system has a robust website (example eviction case: link).  However, one question we want to ask ourselves is whether certain landlords are doing a disproportionate amount of evictions.

 

Right now, that’s a hard question to answer.  We know where most evictions occur, but in many cases, it’s difficult to know who is behind it.  That’s because many landlords who are more professional or full-time use LLC’s, and often register each different address they own to a different LLC.

 

·         Project idea:

Develop a method for being able to say, with some degree of confidence, which properties are really owned by the same person/people/company.

 

·         Supporting data:

One, something similar to this dataset from the Assessor’s Office, but also listing which entity owns each property in Madison.  (Note: I believe it’s possible to obtain a version of this also listing ownership and share it with you; if not, this project may not work.  It’s certainly easy to access on a property-by-property basis online, e.g. this.)

 

Two, I’ve attached an extract of a dataset I obtained under open records law, which shows the ownership of every business with either its agent or office registered in Madison.  It’s from summer 2020, so a bit out of date.  It will not include newer businesses, nor will it include entities that do business in Madison but are registered at a different location.  However, I believe it should include most entities of interest.
